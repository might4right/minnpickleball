Standard scoring

Play to eleven, win by two. No points for receiving; that is true.

A typical pickleball game is played to 11 points and won by two points.

Overtime starts when the score reaches 10-10. The rules for overtime play are the same as the rules for normal play.

Important to pickleball is that only the currently serving team can score points. While this rule can make games a little longer, it also makes for some riveting comebacks and upsets.

Scores are announced before each serve. The server/serving team's score is announced first, then the receiver/receiving team's.

In doubles, a third number, either 1 or 2, indicates which teammate is serving. Refer to "The doubles serving sequence" for more information.

To comply with the doubles serving sequence, the very first serve of a doubles game is always scored 0-0-2. "2," in this case, is the player standing behind the right service court.
