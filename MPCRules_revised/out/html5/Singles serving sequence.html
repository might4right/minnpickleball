Flow of a singles game

The singles serving sequence is very much like other racquet sports.

In singles, once you receive the ball, your first serve will always be from the right court.

If you score a point, both you and your opponent will switch to the other service court, and you will be serving along the opposite diagonal.

If you fault, your opponent gets to serve from their own right service court. When this happens, it is called a side-out.

The general rule of thumb for singles is that you will serve from the right court when your score is even, and from the left court when your score is odd.
