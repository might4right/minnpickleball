Traditional scoring

The traditional mode of pickleball scoring is designed to fit with the flow of the game.

A typical pickleball game is played to 11 points and won by two points. Overtime starts when the score reaches 10-10. It is played by the same rules as normal time.

Important to pickleball is that only the serving team can score points.

Scores are announced before each serve as follows.

  • First, announce the server/serving team's score.
  • Next, announce the receiver/receiving team's score.
  • If you are playing doubles, a third number is included representing which teammate is serving. It can be 1 or 2. Refer to Doubles%20serving%20sequence.html for more information.
  • Remember that the very first serve of a doubles game is always scored 0-0-2. "2" in this case represents the player in the right service court.
